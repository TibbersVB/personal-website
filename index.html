Houssem-Eddine KELLOU

PharmD | PhD Student in Health Economics

UMR 9221 – LEM, CNRS · University of Lille

PhD student in Health Economics at the University of Lille, affiliated with Lille Economie Management (UMR 9221, CNRS), I hold a PharmD and a Master’s degree in Health Economics. My research focuses on the fragmentation of intellectual property in the biopharmaceutical ecosystem and its implications for innovation and access to medicines and vaccines. I work at the intersection of patent analysis and market access, exploring how complex patent landscapes shape competition, pricing, and R&D incentives in the pharmaceutical industry

My thesis examines how fragmented intellectual property structures in the biopharmaceutical sector affect innovation incentives and access to medicines. It explores patent networks, their dynamics, and their impact on market access and R&D investment.
